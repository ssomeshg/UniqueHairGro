Follicle Unit Extraction (FUE)
  • It’s the costliest treatment because it is also the most effective one. Surgeons use a hand-held device to pluck each hair follicle; then, they make incisions in your scalp to implant each hair follicle. It requires multiple sittings that increase the overall cost.
  • On average, an FUE treatment would cost you ₹60,000. Depending on the factors explained above, it can go as high as ₹1,50,000 or as low as ₹25,000.
Follicle Unit Extraction (FUT)
  • FUT is another type of surgical transplant that costs less, but it’s an old technique, and its use has declined because of the FUE hair transplant. In this technique, surgeons cut a small patch of scalp from the back of your head, cut it into small parts, and transplant theminto the affected area. It’s best if you have fewer hairs in the donor area.
  • A FUT treatment will cost you around ₹50,000. The cost may increase or decrease based on multiple factors.
Plasma Rich Platelets (PRP)
  • It’s a three-step non-surgical technique where doctors draw blood from one part of your body, process it, and inject it into your scalp. The plasma-rich blood promotes the strength and growth of your hair.
  • A PRP treatment would cost around ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 in India. But it requires a maintenance injection every six months, which increases the cost.
