Best PRP hair treatment

Process, Benefits, and Side effects.

Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy is a non-surgical treatment where small amount of blood from one part of the body is collected, and spin it in a centrifuge machine, which separates the growth factors from plasma. The acquired plasma is then injected into your scalp to protect the hair follicles and promote hair growth.

Medical science lacks research that justifies the benefits the treatment brings. A few types of research were done with a group of around 40 people and had a success rate of over 90%, but data is limited and cannot assure a guaranteed result.

Even processing and transfusing blood has a severe risk of infections that, if not conducted under experienced doctors, can bring much more harm than good. Who is an ideal candidate?

PRP therapy comes with only a few restrictions, and most may not find themselves eligible for this therapy. It includes blood work that eliminates blood donors with severe diseases, including cancer, liver disease, thyroid, low platelet counts, etc.

Patients facing acute or chronic infections are also ineligible for the process. We consider various factors before injecting you with your blood, most of which can only be known after thoroughly checking your medical history and current health conditions. It’s recommended to connect with a doctor for an exact answer.
